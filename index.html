Hellenistic Astrology

The Ancient Science of the Heavens

Born in the Hellenistic world between 3rd century BCE and 7th century CE, this tradition wove together Babylonian observation, Egyptian decans, and Greek philosophy into the most sophisticated astrological system of the ancient world.

The Foundation

Origins & History

𓂀

Egyptian Roots

The decanic system — dividing the zodiac into 36 segments of 10° each — emerged from ancient Egyptian temple ceilings and star calendars. Egypt's Hellenistic capital, Alexandria, became the cradle of the new synthesis.

𒀭

Babylonian Inheritance

Centuries of meticulous astronomical records from Mesopotamia provided the planetary observations and omens that formed the empirical backbone of Hellenistic astrology. The zodiac itself was a Babylonian invention.

⚛

Greek Philosophy

Stoic cosmology, Platonic metaphysics, and Aristotelian physics gave astrology its theoretical framework. The concept of sympathy (συμπάθεια) — the interconnectedness of all things — underpinned the whole system.

⚖

The Synthesis

Around the 1st–2nd century BCE, the nativity (birth chart) became the primary focus. This shift from mundane omen interpretation to individual fate reading marks the birth of horoscopic astrology as we know it.

The Seven Wanderers

The Classical Planets

Hellenistic astrology recognized seven planets — the "wandering stars" visible to the naked eye. Each carried rich mythological, physical, and temperamental meanings.

☉
The Sun
Ἥλιος · Helios
Sect: Day Domicile: Leo Exaltation: Aries

The luminary of the day sect, the Sun represents the soul, vital spirit, kings, fathers, and public honor. Its position in the chart reveals one's identity and life purpose.

☽
The Moon
Σελήνη · Selene
Sect: Night Domicile: Cancer Exaltation: Taurus

Luminary of the night sect, the Moon governs the body, instincts, mothers, the common people, and all things moist and changeable. She reflects the soul's receptive nature.

☿
Mercury
Ἑρμῆς · Hermes
Sect: Both Domicile: Gemini/Virgo Exaltation: Virgo

The messenger and trickster, Mercury rules the rational mind, speech, writing, commerce, and dexterity. As the only planet of both sects, it adapts its nature to context.

♀
Venus
Ἀφροδίτη · Aphrodite
Sect: Night Domicile: Taurus/Libra Exaltation: Pisces

The lesser benefic, Venus bestows beauty, love, pleasure, arts, and harmony. In a night chart she is especially powerful, bringing grace and good fortune to matters of affection.

♂
Mars
Ἄρης · Ares
Sect: Day Domicile: Aries/Scorpio Exaltation: Capricorn

The lesser malefic, Mars governs conflict, courage, surgery, fire, and iron. In a day chart his heat is tempered; he represents the will to act and the capacity for destruction.

♃
Jupiter
Ζεύς · Zeus
Sect: Day Domicile: Sagittarius/Pisces Exaltation: Cancer

The greater benefic, Jupiter signifies expansion, justice, philosophy, wealth, and divine favor. He is the most fortunate planet, bringing abundance wherever he casts his rays.

♄
Saturn
Κρόνος · Kronos
Sect: Night Domicile: Capricorn/Aquarius Exaltation: Libra

The greater malefic, Saturn rules time, limitation, old age, agriculture, and grief. Yet he also governs wisdom earned through hardship and the discipline that leads to mastery.

The Twelve Places

The Houses (Topoi)

The twelve houses — called topoi (τόποι, "places") in Greek — divide the celestial sphere relative to the horizon. Each house governs a distinct sphere of earthly life.

I
The Helm / Ascendant

Life, the body, character, early years. The most powerful angle — where the soul enters the world.

II
The Gate of Hades

Resources, livelihood, possessions. A weak cadent house, traditionally inauspicious.

III
The Goddess

Siblings, short journeys, communications, religion. Moon-like in its cyclical associations.

IV
The Subterranean Pivot

Parents, home, land, lineage, the end of matters. An angle of deep foundational importance.

V
Good Fortune

Children, pleasure, creativity, erotic love. The Joy of Venus — a place of delight and abundance.

VI
Bad Fortune

Illness, servitude, enemies, animals. The Joy of Mars — a difficult place of hardship.

VII
The Setting Place

Marriage, partnerships, open enemies, contracts. The western angle — powerful and relational.

VIII
The Idle Place

Death, inheritance, surgery, the resources of others. Traditionally weak but intensely karmic.

IX
The God

Philosophy, religion, long journeys, dreams, divination. The Joy of the Sun — exalted matters.

X
The Midheaven

Career, fame, public life, authority. The highest angle — the peak of solar power.

XI
Good Spirit

Friends, hopes, gifts of fortune, patrons. The Joy of Jupiter — a place of beneficence.

XII
Bad Spirit

Exile, hidden enemies, imprisonment, grief. The Joy of Saturn — a place of restriction and sorrow.

The Craft

Key Techniques

The most fundamental organizing principle. A chart is either a day chart (Sun above the horizon) or a night chart (Sun below). Day sect planets (Sun, Jupiter, Saturn) are stronger in day charts; night sect planets (Moon, Venus, Mars) perform better at night.

Five aspects: conjunction (0°), sextile (60°), square (90°), trine (120°), and opposition (180°). These are geometric relationships between signs that "see" each other.

Sensitive points calculated from planet relationships projected from the Ascendant. The Lot of Fortune indicates body and material life; the Lot of Spirit indicates the soul and free will.

Annual Profections: the Ascendant advances one house per year. Zodiacal Releasing produces major and minor periods of activation from the Lots of Fortune and Spirit.

Domicile, Exaltation, Triplicity, Bounds, and Face — five layers of planetary strength based on the sign occupied.

Planets bonified by Jupiter gain benefic influence. Planets under the beams (within 15° of the Sun) are combust. Cazimi (within 16′) is instead a place of great honor.

The Masters

Key Figures

Π

Ptolemy

c. 100–170 CE · Alexandria

Author of the Tetrabiblos (Τετράβιβλος), the most influential astrological text of antiquity.

Β

Vettius Valens

c. 120–175 CE · Antioch

Author of the Anthology (Ἀνθολόγια), the primary source for time-lord techniques including Zodiacal Releasing.

Δ

Dorotheus of Sidon

1st c. CE · Sidon

Author of the Carmen Astrologicum, the foundational text for natal and electional astrology.

Π

Paulus Alexandrinus

4th c. CE · Alexandria

Author of the Introductory Matters (Εἰσαγωγικά), an accessible introductory text preserving many Hellenistic techniques.

Η

Hephaistio of Thebes

5th c. CE

Author of the Apotelesmatika, preserving portions of earlier works now lost.

Μ

Manilius

1st c. CE · Rome

Author of the Astronomica, the earliest surviving complete astrological text in Latin.
